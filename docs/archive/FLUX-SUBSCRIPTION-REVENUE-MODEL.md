### PRICING RATIONALE: $3.99/month

**Why $3.99 instead of $5-10?**

1. **Competitive positioning:**
   - Habitica Premium: $5/month
   - Strides Premium: $4.99/month  
   - Way of Life: $5.99/month
   - **Flux at $3.99** = most affordable premium habit tracker with real money

2. **Psychological pricing:**
   - Under $5 = impulse purchase territory
   - $3.99 feels like $3, not $4
   - "Less than a coffee" positioning
   - Annual = $47.88 (~$4/month) vs. $60-120 for competitors

3. **New player strategy:**
   - Must undercut established players to gain market share
   - Easy to raise prices later (start at $3.99, increase to $4.99 after 10K users)
   - Focus on volume over margin in Year 1

4. **Value perception:**
   - Real banking infrastructure = high perceived value
   - AI chat interface = high perceived value  
   - Behavioral indices = unique value no competitor has
   - $3.99 feels like a steal for this feature set

5. **Conversion optimization:**
   - Lower price = higher conversion rate
   - 50% conversion at $3.99 > 30% conversion at $7.99
   - Math: (50% √ó $3.99) > (30% √ó $7.99) ‚Üí $1.995 ARPU > $2.397 ARPU
   - Actually close, BUT: volume matters more for indices data quality

## PART 3: UNIT ECONOMICS

### CUSTOMER LIFETIME VALUE (LTV)

**Average subscription lifetime:** 24 months (conservative estimate)
- Month 1-6: 65% retention
- Month 7-12: 70% retention
- Month 13+: 75% retention

**Calculation:**
- Monthly revenue per premium user: $3.99
- Expected lifetime: 24 months √ó average retention rate
- Weighted average retention: ~70%
- Expected paid months: 24 √ó 0.70 = 16.8 months
- **LTV = $3.99 √ó 16.8 = $67.03**

**With annual plan discount (20% off = $38.39/year = $3.20/month):**
- If 30% choose annual: Blended ARPU = $3.99(0.70) + $3.20(0.30) = $3.75/month
- LTV = $3.75 √ó 16.8 = $63.00
- But annual plans improve retention to 80%, so: LTV = $3.75 √ó 19.2 = $72.00

**Conservative LTV estimate: $65-72**
